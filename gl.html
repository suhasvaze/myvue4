# Chart of Accounts

# What is Chart of Accounts

As per Traditional Accounting, it is just List of Accounts:

Land, Building , Payables Liability , Receivables , Travel Expenses , Revenue etc

But Fusion offers extended fuctionality of MULTIPLE DIMENSIONS in COA

# COA Dimensions

Wipro India incurrs -- Travel Expenses - for Finance Department
Wipro USA incurrs -- Travel Expenses - for Procurement Department
Wipro Brazil incurrs -- Travel Expenses - for Finance Department

Such examples can be broken into multiple Dimensions as below

Company | Account | Department

Oracle allows usage of Multiple Dimensions like this.

Each Dimension can have its own list.

E.g.

# Company

10 -- Wipro India
20 -- Wipro USA
30 -- Wipro Brasil

# Account

12410 -- Travel Expenses
21540 -- Revenue

# Department

100 -- Finance
200 -- Procurement

And Accounts can be represented as follows

10-12410-100 = Wipro India incurrs -- Travel Expenses - for Finance Department
20-12410-200 = Wipro USA incurrs -- Travel Expenses - for Procurement Department
30-12410-100 = Wipro Brazil incurrs -- Travel Expenses - for Finance Department

This makes Analysis more systematic

# Segment Labels

Different dimensions of COA are called Segments

Some Segments has "Special" meaning. These are assigned with "Segment Labels" E.g.

# Segment Label Examples

# Balancing Segment (Company)
  • Each Value in such segment can maintain Balance Sheet and Profitability separately
  • Journal Balancing must be done for Each Value
  • Mandatory
# Natural Account Segment (Account)
  • Each Value in such segment denotes Asset/Liability/Expense/Revenue/Ownership
  • Mandatory
# Cost Center Segment (Department)
  • Each Value in such segment denotesa Cost / Profit Center
  • Optional

# Ledger

A Ledger can contain "All Journals" of ONE or MULTIPLE Legal Entities

In Traditional Accounting, 
Sometimes word "Ledger" is referred to mean a particular "Ledger A/c" 
like "Travel Expenses", 

But in Fusion, "Ledger" means 
the "Books of Accounts" containing "All Journals"

# Multiple LEs in Single Ledger

In Traditional Accounting we maintain separate "Books of Accounts" for each Legal Entity. But in Fusion, We can maintain one "Ledger" for Multiple Legal Entities (Which use SAME calendar, currency, chart of accounts and accounting method)

Traditional Accounting (Example)

  • Separate Books for Forbes Instruments India (10)
  • Separate Books for Forbes Water Treatment India (20)
  • Separate Books for Forbes Services India (30)

Fusion GL

  • One "Ledger" for (all three Forbes companies) put together
  • If you require, you may maintain "Separate" "Ledgers" for Each or Some of them.

# Journal Posting

# Primary Ledger

Primary Ledger maintains Journals for "All Transactions"

# Secondary Ledger

Secondary Ledger uses the records (Transactions/Journals) of Primary Ledger to interprete the results in a different currency or different Accounting Method

Wipro India (Holding Company)
-- Wipro USA (Subsidiary)
-- Wipro Brasil (Subsidiary)

E.g. In above example, Wipro USA is subsidiary of Wipro India

  • Wipro USA has to primarily generate "Financial Results" as per US GAAP (as it is in USA) -- This will be done by "Primary Ledger"

  • Wipro USA also has to send results to Wipro India (as Wipro India is Holding Company). So Wipro USA also needs to generate "Financial Results" as per "INDIA GAAP" in "INR"

  • Wipro USA can maintain a "Secondary Ledger" to convert its own results into "INDIA GAAP" in "INR"
